# Construction of the Drought Indicator (SPEI)

### Purpose
To capture the physical risk associated with drought conditions, we construct the **Standardized Precipitation–Evapotranspiration Index (SPEI)**, a widely used indicator combining precipitation and atmospheric evaporative demand to measure hydrological deficit.

---

### Data
Monthly climatic variables are obtained from the **SAFRAN reanalysis dataset** produced by Météo-France. SAFRAN provides gridded meteorological variables over metropolitan France at a spatial resolution of approximately 8 km. We use monthly total precipitation together with reference evapotranspiration computed according to the FAO-56 formulation.

---

### Methodology

#### Step 1: Monthly Water Balance
For each grid cell and each month $t$, the monthly water balance is defined as:

$$
D_t = P_t - ET0_t
$$

where $P_t$ denotes total precipitation and $ET0_t$ the reference evapotranspiration.

#### Step 2: Cumulative Water Balance
The drought indicator is constructed from the cumulative water balance over a **six-month rolling window**:

$$
D_t^{(6)} = \sum_{i=0}^{5} D_{t-i}
$$

Following the standard SPEI methodology, the series $D_t^{(6)}$ is fitted using a log-logistic distribution, and the cumulative probabilities are transformed into standardized normal values.

---

### Spatial Aggregation
The index is first computed at the commune level. To obtain a national-level indicator, the monthly SPEI values are averaged across all communes:

$$
\overline{SPEI}_t = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} SPEI_{t,i}
$$

where:
* $i$ indexes communes.
* $N$ denotes the number of spatial units.

The final series represents the monthly intensity of drought conditions at the national scale and is used as a proxy for physical risk related to soil moisture deficit and hydrological stress.
